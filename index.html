📐 The Full Formula — explained for humans

Step 1 — How the 11 stocks got picked (the IVQM screen, score out of 100)

Every NIFTY 500 stock was pushed through four filters. Think of them as four questions:

VALUE — 30 pts"Am I paying little for a lot?" Low P/E (price ÷ yearly earnings per share), low price-to-book, fat dividend, and a price well below its 52-week high.
QUALITY — 30 pts"Is this a good business?" ROCE above 15-20% (how much profit the company squeezes from its capital), profits actually growing, little or no debt.
MOMENTUM — 25 pts"Has the market started agreeing with me?" Price above its 200-day average, 50-day average above the 200-day, beating the NIFTY over 6-12 months. Cheap + rising beats cheap + falling.
SAFETY — 15 pts"Can this blow up?" Zero promoter share-pledging, strong promoter holding, big-enough market cap, low debt. In India this filter matters most.

Why these weights? Decades of data: cheap-stock strategies and momentum strategies each beat the market alone, but combined they roughly double the risk-adjusted return (AQR, Journal of Finance 2013; Capitalmind ran the same idea on NSE 2007-2023: ₹100 became ₹1,921 vs ₹448 in the Nifty). The quality + safety half is the value-trap insurance — in India, the Piotroski quality checklist is the single best filter for avoiding cheap stocks that deserve to be cheap.

Step 2 — How the "20-30% by December" number is computed (the three engines)

A share price is not magic. It is always just two numbers multiplied:

PRICE = EPS × P/E
(what the company earns per share) × (how many rupees the market pays for each rupee of earnings)

So there are only three ways a stock pays you, and we add all three:

Expected price = EPS × (1 + growth) × fair P/E
Expected return = (Expected price ÷ today’s price − 1) + dividends

Engine 1 — earnings grow. If EPS rises 10%, the price rises ~10% even if nothing else changes. Engine 2 — the multiple normalizes. These stocks trade at P/E 5-14 while their own history and peers say 6-17 is normal. When fear fades, the multiple mean-reverts — that alone is 10-25%. Engine 3 — dividends. Several picks pay 4-6% a year in cash; half a year ≈ 2-3%. For banks we use book value × P/B instead (a bank is a pile of money; P/B below 1 means buying ₹1 for 80 paise).

Crucially, the growth numbers used below are deliberately LOWER than what the companies just reported, and the "fair" multiples are at or below each stock’s own historical average. The 20-30% target needs no heroics — it needs things to merely stop being abnormal.

Step 4 — How sure? Honest probabilities, not promises

The arithmetic above is exact; the assumptions (growth, fair multiple) are judgment. So each stock gets a probability that the +20% actually lands before Dec 31, 2026. It is built from five things: how small the re-rating ask is, whether the trend is already up (above the 200-DMA roughly doubles the odds vs a falling knife), how hard and dated the catalyst is, how visible the earnings are, and the dividend floor under the price. Calibration anchor: in flat-to-rising Indian markets, value+momentum names hit +20% in 6 months roughly half the time; in falling markets rarely. These are structured estimates — not backtested guarantees.

The portfolio is the real bet. If each of the 8 picks has ~50% odds, basic binomial math says: chance that at least 4 of 8 gain 20%+ ≈ 64%; at least 3 of 8 ≈ 86%; all 8 ≈ under 1%. One big caveat: the picks are correlated through the index — a NIFTY rally lifts most of them together, a crash sinks most together. NIFTY direction is roughly half of the outcome, and nobody can promise that half.

Step 5 — The technical toolkit (the exact formulas in the per-stock panel)

RSI(14) = 100 − 100 ÷ (1 + avg gain ÷ avg loss)   over the last 14 trading days
under 30 = beaten up (oversold) · 40-65 = healthy · over 70 = frothy (overbought)
MACD = EMA12 − EMA26  ·  signal = EMA9 of MACD  ·  histogram = MACD − signal
EMA = exponential moving average. Histogram above zero = short-term push is bullish; below = bearish
200-DMA = average closing price of the last 200 days
the institutional line in the sand: above it, funds buy dips; below it, they sell rallies. 50-DMA above 200-DMA = "Stage 2" uptrend
Bollinger position = (price − lower band) ÷ (upper − lower) × 100,  bands = 20-day avg ± 2σ
upper half = strength; hugging the lower band = weakness. σ = standard deviation of the last 20 closes
Volume ratio = 20-day avg volume ÷ 90-day avg volume
above 1.0 = money flowing in NOW vs its own normal; the fuel behind any breakout
What can go wrong (read this twice): These are assumptions, not promises. Screens like this beat the index in ~59% of 1-year windows historically — meaning 4 years in 10 they lag. Roughly half the individual picks work; the portfolio wins because winners run further than losers fall. The multiple may never re-rate if the reason it’s cheap turns out to be real (AI for IT, energy transition for coal). Keep each position ≤5%, and follow the sell rules: 15+ sessions below the 200-DMA, any promoter pledge appears, promoter sells more than 2%, or the composite score decays below ~55.
